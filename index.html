Who We Are

I am4ProfNaanaJane is a volunteer group of young dynamic Ghanaian professionals who are eager to see a better Ghana, free of the corruption and waste that the country has experienced over the past four years. The group has been set up primarily to increase the name recognition and develop affection for Prof. Naana Jane Opoku-Agyeman ahead of the 2020 elections in her candidature as presidential running mate for the largest opposition party in Ghana. Our Goals are;

  • To actively promote the brand of Prof. Naana Jane
  • Countering any ‘fake’ news or inaccurate information
  • To build acceptance and strong voter support among key voting groups
  • Organizing virtual and online fundraising events
  • Canvassing on behalf of the campaigns to key associations and unions

Our Vision

To strengthen the political brand for Prof. Naana Jane that is closely aligned with the core values of the NDC and based on her principles of diversity, integrity, transparency and collaboration. Ultimately, creating a positive relationship between Prof. Naana Jane and Ghanaian voters ahead of the 2020 elections and beyond.

Our Mission

To rally behind Prof. Naana Jane Opoku-Agyemang; elevating her political profile and name recognition nationwide ahead of the 2020 elections, mobilizing voter support for her and John Dramani Mahama’s campaign, and positioning the NDC party as the leader in female political participation & representation and issues of importance for female voters.

Her Story

Professor Opoku-Agyemang taught and worked at the University of Cape Coast, starting in 1986. She has held various academic positions including Head of the Department of English, Dean of the Faculty of Arts, Warden of Adehye Hall, Valco Trust Fund Post-Graduate Hostel, and Dean of School of Graduate Studies and Research. From 1997, she has held the position of Academic Director of the School for International Training in the History and Cultures of the African Diaspora. From 2008 to 2012 she was the University's Vice-Chancellor. In March, 2007, she was one of five scholars selected to deliver presentations during the 200th Anniversary of the Abolition of Slavery at the United Nations Headquarters in New York City.

In October 2009, she was elected Ghana's representative to the Executive Board of the United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organisation (UNESCO). Ahead of the 2012 general elections, Jane Opoku Agyemang moderated the debate with Kojo Oppong Nkrumah. Between February 2013 and January 2017 she served as the Education Minister of Ghana. On 26 October 2018, she became the Chancellor of the Women's University in Africa located in Zimbabwe. She has served on many local and international boards and committees such as the Centre for Democratic Governance, (CDD-Ghana), the Editorial Board of The Harriet Tubman Series on the African Diaspora (Africa World Press Inc. USA), the Africa Initiative in Canada, and the College of Physicians and Surgeons as Eminent Citizen.

Between February 2013 and January 2017 she served as the Education Minister of Ghana. Opoku-Agyemang was selected as the presidential running mate for the National Democratic Congress (NDC) on July 6, 2020 for Ghana’s December 2020 General elections
